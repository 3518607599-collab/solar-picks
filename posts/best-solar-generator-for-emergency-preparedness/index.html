Preparation Tips

  1. Pre-charge your generator — Keep it at 80% charge. LiFePO4 batteries lose only 3% per month.
  2. Test quarterly — Run your essentials for an hour to make sure everything works.
  3. Store panels accessible — Don't bury them in the attic. You need them ready to deploy.
  4. Know your wattage — Label your appliances with their wattage so you can quickly calculate what to run.
  5. Have a charging plan — Know where you'll place panels for maximum sun exposure at your home.

Solar Generator vs Gas Generator for Emergencies

Factor Solar Generator Gas Generator
Indoor use ✅ Yes ❌ No (CO poisoning risk)
Noise Silent 65-80 dB (angry neighbors)
Fuel storage None needed Gas goes stale, needs rotation
Fuel cost Free $20-50/day
Maintenance Zero Oil, spark plugs, carburetor
Runtime Unlimited with solar Until gas runs out
Reliability Always works Won't start if not maintained

Solar generators are objectively better for most emergency situations. The only scenario where gas wins is extended overcast weather combined with high power needs — and even then, the best solution is both: solar for daily use, gas as backup.
