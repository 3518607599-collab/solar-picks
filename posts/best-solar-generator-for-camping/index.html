Best Solar Generator for Camping 2026: 5 Tested Picks for Every Budget

Camping doesn't mean going without power anymore. A portable solar generator keeps your phones charged, your lights on, and your food cold — all from sunlight. Here are our tested picks for every camping style and budget.

What Do You Need Power for While Camping?

Essential:

  • Phone charging (10W × 2 phones × 3h = 60Wh)
  • LED lantern (10W × 5h = 50Wh)
  • Total: ~110Wh/day

Comfortable:

  • Above essentials
  • Portable fan (20W × 8h = 160Wh)
  • Bluetooth speaker (10W × 4h = 40Wh)
  • Camera/drone charging (50Wh)
  • Total: ~360Wh/day

Glamping:

  • Above comfortable
  • Mini fridge (60W × 12h = 720Wh)
  • Laptop (65W × 3h = 195Wh)
  • Portable projector (50W × 2h = 100Wh)
  • Total: ~1,375Wh/day

Pro Camping Power Tips

  1. Charge everything before you leave — Start your trip at 100%
  2. Place panels in direct sun by 9 AM — Morning charging is crucial
  3. Use DC outputs when possible — USB-C and DC ports are more efficient than AC
  4. Turn off the inverter when not using AC — The inverter uses 5-10W even with nothing plugged in
  5. Keep the generator in shade — Batteries perform better when cool
  6. Bring a car charger — Charge while driving between campsites
