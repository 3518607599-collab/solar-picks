Living in an RV full-time means you need reliable power — for your fridge, lights, devices, and sometimes even air conditioning. Here's our tested guide to the best solar generators for RV life.

How Much Power Does an RV Need?

Before buying a solar generator, understand your power needs:

Appliance Watts Hours/Day Daily Wh
RV Refrigerator 60W 24h 1,440 Wh
LED Lights (6x) 30W 5h 150 Wh
Laptop 65W 4h 260 Wh
Phone Charging 10W 3h 30 Wh
Fan 40W 8h 320 Wh
Water Pump 50W 1h 50 Wh
Total 2,250 Wh/day

A small RV with efficient appliances needs about 2,000-2,500Wh per day. Add air conditioning and that jumps to 5,000-8,000Wh.

Installation Tips for RV Solar

  1. Mount panels on the roof — Permanent roof-mounted panels charge while you drive and when parked. Use tilt mounts for 15-20% more output.
  2. Use an Anderson connector — Most solar generators accept Anderson input. Run a cable from your roof panels to an exterior Anderson port.
  3. Position your RV south-facing — When parked, orient your RV so the roof panels face south (in the Northern Hemisphere).
  4. Add a DC-DC charger — Charge your solar generator from your vehicle's alternator while driving. This adds 300-500Wh per hour of driving.
